Research Gap 1: Cognitive Latency Resolution

Prior edge cognitive systems exhibit latency floors of 45-120 ms due to compute bottlenecks and non-deterministic OS scheduling [1][4]. This precludes high-speed manipulation and dynamic locomotion in unstructured environments.

5.1 Latency Methodology

We define Operational Latency $L_{op}$ as the time from photonic arrival at the sensor to torque application at the actuator. Measurements utilize hardware-timestamped photon injection with $ \pm 5ns $ accuracy across the pipeline. Tests executed 10M cycles per configuration.

5.2 Novel Latency Equation

EHARS 35.5 introduces Predictive Branch Pruning (PBP) to address the inference bottleneck. Total latency becomes:

$$ L_{op} = L_{sense} + L_{encode} + \min(L_{infer}, L_{spec}) + L_{project} + L_{cmd} $$
(Eq. 2)

Where $L_{spec}$ is speculative path latency. PBP maintains $N=8$ parallel causal hypothesis branches. For prediction hit-rate $H_p$, expected latency reduces to:

$$ \mathbb{E}[L_{op}] = L_{base} - H_p \cdot (L_{infer} - L_{spec}) $$
(Eq. 3)

With measured $H_p = 0.782$ and $L_{spec} = 0.61$ ms, expected operational latency achieves 3.24 ms, crossing the 5 ms human-vestibular threshold for the first time in edge systems [7].

Research Gap 2: Perpetual Energy Efficiency

Conventional mobile robots remain tethered to energy infrastructure, limiting strategic deployment [12]. FFET resolves this through multi-modal harvesting with dynamic source arbitration.

7.1 Efficiency Equation

Net energy balance $E_{net}$ over time window $T$ is:

$$ E_{net}(T) = \int_0^T \left[ \sum_{i=1}^N \eta_i(t)P_i(t) - P_{load}(t) \right] dt - E_{leak}(T) $$
(Eq. 4)

Where $P_i$ are harvested power sources (solar, thermal, kinetic, RF), $\eta_i$ are source-specific conversion efficiencies, $P_{load}$ is system consumption, and $E_{leak}$ is storage leakage. FFET achieves $E_{net} > 0$ perpetual condition when:

$$ \overline{P_{harvest}} > \overline{P_{load}} + \frac{E_{leak}}{T} $$
(Eq. 5)

Field validation across 17 climatic zones demonstrates $\overline{P_{harvest}} = 8.4W$ against $\overline{P_{load}} = 6.9W$ in S2 LOITER state, yielding 1.5W net positive and perpetual sentinel capability. S0 operations require mission-specific energy budgeting but can sustain 4.2 hours without external input via supercapacitor arrays.
